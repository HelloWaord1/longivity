# Phosphatidylserine (PS)

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** B
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Phospholipid that is a major component of cell membranes, particularly concentrated in brain tissue. Supports cognitive function, cortisol regulation, and cell signaling. FDA-qualified health claim for cognitive decline risk reduction. Levels decline with age.

## Mechanisms of Action
- Cell membrane fluidity and integrity
- Cortisol regulation (blunts excess)
- Acetylcholine and dopamine support
- Apoptotic signaling (cell membrane asymmetry)

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 100-300mg/day
- **Range:** 100-600mg/day
- **Notes:** Soy or sunflower derived. FDA-qualified health claim for dementia risk reduction (2003). Take with meals (fat-soluble). May help cortisol management for athletes.

## Key Findings
- Improved cognitive function in elderly with memory complaints (multiple RCTs)
- FDA-qualified health claim for reduced dementia risk
- Blunted cortisol response to exercise stress in athletes

## Interactions
- Blood thinners (mild anticoagulant effect)
- Anticholinergic medications

## Side Effects
- GI discomfort
- Insomnia (if taken late)

## Contraindications
- Bleeding disorders

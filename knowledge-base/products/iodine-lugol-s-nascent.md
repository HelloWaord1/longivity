# Iodine (Lugol's / Nascent)

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** B
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Essential trace element critical for thyroid function, metabolism, and cellular health. Deficiency is widespread globally and linked to cognitive decline, metabolic dysfunction, and increased cancer risk. Supports thyroid hormone production (T3/T4).

## Mechanisms of Action
- Thyroid hormone synthesis (T3, T4)
- Breast and prostate tissue health
- Antioxidant in thyroid (via thyroid peroxidase)
- Cellular detoxification (displaces halides)

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 150-300mcg/day
- **Range:** 150mcg-12.5mg/day

## Key Findings
- 2 billion people worldwide are iodine deficient (WHO)
- Japanese dietary intake (~1-3mg/day) associated with lower breast cancer rates
- Essential for cognitive development and maintenance throughout life

## Interactions
- Thyroid medications (levothyroxine)
- Lithium
- Anti-thyroid drugs

## Side Effects
- Thyroid dysfunction if excessive
- Metallic taste
- Skin breakouts (bromide detox)

## Contraindications
- Hashimoto thyroiditis (use with caution, low dose)
- Hyperthyroidism
- Thyroid nodules

# Boron

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** C
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Trace mineral that supports bone health, hormone metabolism, and brain function. Increases free testosterone by reducing SHBG, supports vitamin D metabolism, and has anti-inflammatory properties. Often overlooked but increasingly recognized in longevity circles.

## Mechanisms of Action
- SHBG reduction (increases free testosterone)
- Vitamin D metabolism enhancement
- Bone health (reduces calcium and magnesium excretion)
- Anti-inflammatory (reduces hs-CRP and TNF-α)

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 3-6mg/day
- **Range:** 3-10mg/day
- **Notes:** Boron citrate, glycinate, or fructoborate forms. Found in avocados, nuts, dried fruits. Very affordable. Take with meals.

## Key Findings
- Increased free testosterone by 28% and reduced estradiol in 1-week supplementation study
- Reduced inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, TNF-α) within 1 week
- Populations with low boron intake have higher arthritis prevalence

## Interactions
- Estrogen therapy (may modulate estrogen metabolism)

## Side Effects
- Minimal at recommended doses
- GI discomfort at very high doses

## Contraindications
- Kidney impairment (reduced excretion)

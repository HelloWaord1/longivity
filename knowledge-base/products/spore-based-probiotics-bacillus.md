# Spore-Based Probiotics (Bacillus)

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** B
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Spore-forming Bacillus strains (B. subtilis, B. coagulans, B. clausii) that survive stomach acid and colonize the gut more effectively than standard probiotics. Function as "reconditioning" probiotics that modulate the existing microbiome rather than colonizing permanently.

## Mechanisms of Action
- Gut microbiome reconditioning
- Short-chain fatty acid production
- Gut barrier integrity improvement
- Competitive exclusion of pathogens

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 1-2 capsules/day
- **Range:** Product-specific CFU counts

## Key Findings
- Reduced metabolic endotoxemia (LPS leakage) in randomized trial
- Survived gastric acid and colonized colon (vs <1% survival of standard probiotics)
- Improved gut barrier function and reduced inflammatory markers

## Interactions
- Antibiotics (take 2hr apart)
- Immunosuppressants (theoretical)

## Side Effects
- Initial die-off symptoms (bloating, gas)
- GI adjustment period

## Contraindications
- Severe immunosuppression
- Short bowel syndrome

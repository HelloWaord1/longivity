# Luteolin

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** C
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Flavonoid found in celery, peppers, and chamomile with potent anti-neuroinflammatory and mast cell stabilizing properties. Crosses the blood-brain barrier and inhibits microglial activation, making it particularly relevant for brain aging and neuroinflammation.

## Mechanisms of Action
- Mast cell stabilization
- Microglial inhibition
- NF-κB suppression
- Nrf2 activation

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 100-200mg/day
- **Range:** 50-400mg/day
- **Notes:** Liposomal form for better absorption. Found in celery seed, chamomile, peppers. Often combined with rutin for synergy.

## Key Findings
- Potent inhibitor of microglial activation and neuroinflammation
- Improved cognitive function in brain fog and post-viral syndromes
- Crosses blood-brain barrier — one of few flavonoids that does effectively

## Interactions
- CYP3A4 and CYP1A2 substrates (may inhibit metabolism)

## Side Effects
- Mild GI upset
- Headache (rare)

## Contraindications
- Pregnancy (insufficient data)

# Vitamin K2 (MK-7)

**Category:** supplement
**Evidence Grade:** B
**Risk Profile:** low

## Description
Fat-soluble vitamin essential for calcium metabolism — directs calcium to bones and teeth while preventing arterial calcification. MK-7 form (from natto) has longest half-life and best clinical evidence. Critical companion to vitamin D3 supplementation.

## Mechanisms of Action
- Matrix GLA protein activation (prevents arterial calcification)
- Osteocalcin activation (directs calcium to bones)
- Vitamin D3 synergy
- Mitochondrial electron transport participation

## Dosage
- **Standard:** 100-200mcg MK-7/day
- **Range:** 100-500mcg/day
- **Notes:** Always pair with vitamin D3. MK-7 form preferred (longer half-life than MK-4). Fat-soluble — take with meals. Essential if supplementing high-dose D3.

## Key Findings
- Reduced arterial stiffness in postmenopausal women (3-year RCT)
- MK-7 supplementation improved bone density and reduced fractures
- Rotterdam Study: highest K2 intake associated with 50% less arterial calcification

## Interactions
- Warfarin (CONTRAINDICATED — directly antagonizes warfarin)
- Other anticoagulants

## Side Effects
- Minimal
- GI discomfort (rare)

## Contraindications
- Warfarin/coumarin anticoagulant therapy
- Hypercoagulable states
